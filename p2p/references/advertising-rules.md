# P2P Advertising Rules

## Platform Comparison

| Dimension | YouTube | Facebook/Instagram |
|-----------|---------|-------------------|
| Attention | Must watch 5 sec (unskippable) | Can scroll past instantly |
| Key goal | Get past 5-sec skip button | STOP THE SCROLL |
| Biggest drop-off | First 30 seconds | First 2-3 seconds |
| CTA length | Longer = better (500-1000% improvement) | Shorter = better (ad stays on screen) |
| After ad ends | YouTube plays viewer's video in 3 sec | Ad stays on screen |
| Policy | More lenient | MUCH stricter (account bans) |
| Paying | Don't pay if skip before 20 sec | Pay per impression/click |

## "What Are You Really Selling?" Assessment

Before writing ANY ad, determine what you're actually selling:

| Ad Type | What You're Selling |
|---------|---------------------|
| E-commerce product page | The product / solution to their problem |
| Event / webinar registration | The desire to attend / be part of it |
| Lead magnet (PDF, quiz) | The desire to consume that free content |
| VSL / free training | **CURIOSITY** -- getting them curious about what's in the video |

What you're selling changes completely how you approach the ad.

## 3 Audience Temperatures

| Temperature | Who | Approach |
|-------------|-----|----------|
| **Cold** | Never heard of you | Full structure: verify --> validate --> philosophical problem --> explain --> solve |
| **Warm** | Know you, haven't bought | More direct, re-engage, remind about problems/solutions |
| **Hot** | Very close to buying | Offers, urgency, scarcity, testimonials, social proof |

## YouTube Ad Structure (15 Steps)

1. **Provocative opening** (first 5 seconds -- unskippable)
2. **Present a duality** (create conflict)
3. **Ask "Why?"** (why are people stuck)
4. **Give the reason** (explanation)
5. **Who you are + credibility** (briefly)
6. **Allay their fears** ("it doesn't have to be that way")
7. **Early weak CTA** (before 60 seconds -- seed the action)
8. **Show what happens after click** (show landing page -- removes fear of unknown)
9. **Benefit + remove pain** ("you can do this WITHOUT having X")
10. **Testimonials** ("I can't guarantee, but here's what these people did")
11. **Stack benefits** (convince remaining viewers)
12. **Urgency/scarcity** (reason to act NOW)
13. **Longer final CTA** (comprehensive)
14. **Leave screen with USP + click arrows for 7-10 sec** (give time to click)
15. **People click WHILE ad plays** (once it ends, they're gone in 3 sec)

### YouTube CTA Rules
- ALWAYS have CTA before 60 seconds
- Don't end the video after CTA -- keep going
- Long CTA = 500-1000% performance improvement
- After your ad ends, YouTube plays their video within 3 seconds

### 20-Second Filter Rule
Before 20 seconds, filter out non-target audience. Non-target people skip = you don't pay. Free brand awareness for target audience.

## Facebook Ad Structure

### Stop-Scroll Elements
- Obnoxious, attention-grabbing visuals
- Pattern interrupts ("Stop scrolling for a second and listen up")
- Very dynamic video with rapid shot changes
- You must be "almost obnoxious" to stop the scroll

### Structure
1. Stop the scroll (visual + textual hook)
2. Hook in 2-3 seconds (biggest drop-off point)
3. Fast pace throughout
4. Short, punchy CTA (ad stays on screen)

### Facebook Image Ads
When selling curiosity: no video needed. Just attention-grabbing image + short copy + quick CTA.
Keep headline to one line (so sub-headline appears).

### Facebook Policy (CRITICAL)
Things that get ads REJECTED or ACCOUNT BANNED:
- Keywords like "weight loss" directly
- Specific money claims (dollar amounts)
- Addressing personal attributes ("you're experiencing back pain")
- Health-related claims
- Results-based claims that won't apply to every single viewer

**Policy Test:** "Is this claim true for EVERY SINGLE person who reads this ad?" If no --> Facebook considers it misleading.

**Workarounds:**
- Use abstractions -- talk about the thing without mentioning it
- Use analogies (visual and textual)
- Use emojis in place of flagged words
- Big spenders get special treatment (dedicated Facebook contact)

## 8 Headline Criteria (for Landing Pages & Ads)

Every headline must pass these:

| # | Criteria | Test |
|---|---------|------|
| 1 | Appeals to Self-Interest | Does the reader immediately see personal benefit? |
| 2 | Implies Proof of Promise | Are there third-party sources or mechanisms as proof? |
| 3 | Gives Hope | Can they see others like them succeeding? |
| 4 | Creates Tension (Conflict) | Does it use dualities? |
| 5 | Piques Intellectual Interest | Is it something NEW they haven't heard? |
| 6 | Triggers Dominant Emotion | Does it elicit greed, fear, curiosity? |
| 7 | Is Believable | Big claim + big proof point = believable? |
| 8 | Is Dramatized | Is the language dramatic, not flat? |

## Ad Scent (Congruence)

Landing page MUST match the ad. If ad mentions a Christmas sale, landing page must too. Disconnect = they leave. Majority of conversion rate = congruence.

## CTA Button Best Practices

Write as if the READER is saying it:
- "I want to watch the case study now" > "Watch case study"
- "Start my free trial" > "Sign up"
- Formula: [Action Verb] + [What They Get]

## Key Principle

> "The product is NOT the solution. The product is the VEHICLE that allows you to EXPERIENCE the solution. First explain the solution, THEN present the product as what enables it."
