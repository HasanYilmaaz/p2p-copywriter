# P2P Long-Form / Launch Canvas

## The Launch Canvas (Fill BEFORE Writing)

### Step 1: Problem & Desire
Center promotion around ONE desire and ONE problem ("Principle of the One Thing").

Fill all layers:
- **Desire** (surface level) -- e.g., "create $10K/month online business"
- **External Problem** -- e.g., working a hated job
- **Internal Problem** -- e.g., losing precious time of life
- **Philosophical Problem** -- e.g., knows it's possible but doesn't know how
- **Real Desire** -- e.g., reclaim ownership of life

### Step 2: Duality & Common Enemy
- Create "us vs. them" narrative
- Identify Common Enemy to throw rocks at
- Name Top of Mountain (what they become when they succeed)
- Name Rock Bottom (what they become if they don't act)

Examples:
- "New Renaissance Man" vs. "Modern Day Slave" (enemy: Puppet Masters)
- "Picture Perfect Fit" vs. "Prisoner of the Plate" (enemy: Traditional depriving diets)
- "Master of your own future" vs. "Slave of your fears" (enemy: Conventional path)

### Step 3: Marketing Thesis (One Belief Formula)

> "This NEW OPPORTUNITY is the key to THEIR DESIRE, and it's only attainable through MY NEW MECHANISM."

Examples:
- **P90X:** "Avoiding the plateau effect is the key to building muscle, attainable only through muscle confusion"
- **Invisalign:** "Aligning teeth without ugly braces is the key to getting the smile you want, attainable only through Invisalign technology"
- **Subway:** "Eating healthy fast food is the key to losing weight, attainable only through Subway sandwiches"

### Step 4: Big Idea

> "The single biggest reason for poor conversion is the lack of one big, compelling idea that ties the promotion together." -- Todd Brown

**Big Idea Checklist:**
1. Emotionally Compelling (must elicit emotion)
2. Contains Primary Promise (implied or explicit benefit)
3. Intellectually Interesting ("I need to know more")
4. Connects familiar concepts in unfamiliar ways ("best kept secret hidden in plain sight")

**Examples:**
- BAD: "How I Started My Online Marketing Agency" (no big idea)
- GOOD: "Outlawed for 41 years, now legal. This investment could return 665%"
- GOOD: "There's a new railroad across America making people rich"

Big ideas emerge from research + stepping away (walk, gym). NOT from sitting and thinking.

### Step 5: Offer Pillars

Think in PILLARS, not features/benefits. Each pillar fills a gap your narrative creates.

Process:
1. Go back to marketing thesis
2. Ask: what does someone need to become [top of mountain]?
3. Each requirement = a pillar
4. Each pillar maps to a component of your offer

Example (Educate launch):
- Pillar 1: Knowledge & Business --> Course
- Pillar 2: Community --> Student community
- Pillar 3: Accountability --> Expert coaches + Q&As
- Pillar 4: Self-improvement --> Bonus course

**Seed the need for your product from the VERY FIRST MINUTE** (not just at the pitch).

### Step 6: Chain of Beliefs

Reverse-engineer from purchase decision:

1. End goal: they buy your offer
2. Before that: they must believe the marketing thesis
3. Before THAT: what do they need to believe? Work backwards until you meet them where they ARE

**Questions to generate beliefs:**
1. What must they believe about YOU?
2. What must they believe about THEMSELVES? (Most critical -- they doubt THEY can do it)
3. What must they believe about the MARKETING THESIS?
4. What must they believe about OTHER SOLUTIONS? (in sophisticated markets)

**Rules:**
- "A claim without a proof point is just a claim. Claims WITH proof points create beliefs."
- It's a CHAIN -- break one link, entire chain breaks
- Every argument must move forward (Jordan Belfort's Straight Line)
- "We are not selling, we are solving."

### Step 7: Lead Type

**Eugene Schwartz's 5 Levels of Awareness:**
1. Unaware (not aware of problem or solution)
2. Problem Aware (knows problem, not solutions)
3. Solution Aware (knows problem + solutions, hasn't decided)
4. Product Aware (knows solution types, not YOUR product)
5. Most Aware (knows you, competition, everything)

**Lead Types (matched to awareness):**

| Awareness | Lead Type | Description |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Most Aware | Offer Lead | Lead with product, price, discounts, guarantees |
| Most Aware | Invitation Lead | Subtle offer, sounds exclusive |
| Solution Aware | Promise Lead | Lead with results promise, not product |
| Problem Aware | Problem-Solution Lead | Lead with problem, pivot to solution |
| Less Aware | Secret/System Lead | "Best kept secret hidden in plain sight" |
| Unaware | Story Lead | Narrative that draws them in before revealing the problem |

Rule: More aware = more direct. Less aware = more indirect. Any lead for less aware also works for more aware (not vice versa).

The lead's primary job: SELL THE VIEWERSHIP (get them to watch the whole thing).

## Anchoring / NLP Concept

Use abstract words ("conventional path," "modern-day slave") and spend the ENTIRE promotion creating meaning for them. Once meaning is created, just mentioning the word triggers all the emotion you built.

## YouTube Optimization

### Two Key Metrics
- **CTR:** Target 10% day 1, ~5% long-term
- **Retention:** Target 35-60%

High CTR + low retention = PUNISHED (clickbait)
High CTR + high retention = PROMOTED aggressively

### Packaging Strategy
- Think about packaging BEFORE writing
- Broad topics > niche specifics
- "Best online business model for beginners" (broad, millions of views) > "How to start a marketing agency" (niche, limited)

### Title Rules
- Short, broad, punchy, somewhat click-baity
- NOT long copywriting headlines
- Study MrBeast, Mark Rober, Veritasium

### Thumbnail Rules
- Simple, 1-3 elements max
- Stills often beat manipulated photos
- Create at least 5 per video and test

### Evergreen vs Event-Based
- **Evergreen:** Timeless concepts, update year in title annually, generates views for years
- **Event-Based:** Trending/timely topics, designed for 1-week spike

## Key VSL Tactics

1. **"Big Relevant Change" Opening:** Name a change --> winners and losers --> path to winning
2. **Loop Opening:** Tease info, promise later ("More about that in a minute")
3. **Damaging Admission + Takeaway Sale:** "I can't guarantee your results" --> increases trust + curiosity
4. **Relatable Testimonials:** Average people, NOT geniuses. "If THEY can do it, I definitely can"
5. **Contrarian Disqualification:** Create objective criteria --> show only your model meets all criteria
6. **Self-introduction AFTER the hook** (never start with intro)
7. **Selling the Viewership:** Every element in the lead exists to keep them watching
