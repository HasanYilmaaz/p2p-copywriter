# P2P Copy Formulas & Structures

## The 5 Basic Elements (Gather Before Writing ANYTHING)

### 1. Target Customer
- Be specific but not absurdly narrow
- Use demographics (age, location, gender) + behaviors (what they want to do)
- When you call them out specifically, they say "Oh, that's me!"
- Example: "Women over 35 from London" or "18-25 year olds wanting to start a business"

### 2. Desired Outcome
- What the target customer wants to achieve
- THE MORE SPECIFIC, THE BETTER
- NOT "lose weight" --> "lose 3 kilos of belly fat"
- NOT "make money" --> "make $10,000/month"
- Find the specific number/thing in their head that represents their desired outcome

### 3. Benefit
- What makes the desired outcome even MORE enticing
- Added on TOP of the desired outcome (usually time or ease)
- "Lose 3 kilos of belly fat... IN UNDER 45 DAYS"
- "Make $10,000/month... WITHOUT any previous experience"

### 4. Pain Point
- Biggest obstacle/frustration when trying to achieve desired outcome
- Two sides of the same coin as benefits
- Address pain points to handle objections BEFORE they arise
- Example: starvation diets, giving up favorite foods, investing money upfront

### 5. Signature Solution
- Give your solution a UNIQUE NAME that sets it apart from all competition
- DON'T use category words (don't call it a "diet" -- they'll compare to all diets)
- DO use different category words: "protocol," "system," "challenge," "method"
- "Every Other Day Protocol" > "Every Other Day Diet"
- This prevents automatic comparison shopping in the reader's mind

## The 6-Step Formula (PRIMARY STRUCTURE)

The only structure you need for high-converting copy:

### Step 1: VERIFY
Start with something they have experienced, seen, or heard before. This gets your message through the self-sealing logic bubble.

> "Does the thought of taking photos fill you with dread?"

### Step 2: VALIDATE INTERNAL PROBLEM
Address how they're feeling (emotional connection).

> "This isn't a passing feeling. It permeates your thoughts, affects your ability to enjoy the moment..."

### Step 3: PIVOT TO PHILOSOPHICAL PROBLEM
Introduce the duality/conflict. This triggers stories inside the reader's head.

> "They tried low-carb, keto, caloric restriction -- that's exactly the problem. Restricting diets only make you gain MORE weight long-term."

The duality: what helps them = what stops them (the flip).

### Step 4: EXPLANATION
Provide logical reason WHY (emotion drives decision, logic justifies it).

> "Here's why: they slow down metabolism and turn on hunger hormones"

### Step 5: BENEFIT
They're ready to decide -- make it irresistible.

> "When you understand how ghrelin and leptin work, you never need a starvation diet again"

### Step 6: CALL TO ACTION
Tell them exactly what to do. Every message must end with a CTA.

> "If you want to find out how, I explain it in this video [link]"

**Connecting words between segments:** "And worse yet," "Truth is," "Here's why," "The fact is," "Here's the thing," "But here's what most people don't realize"

## PWAS (Problem - Why - Agitate - Solve)

1. **Problem** -- Lead with a specific problem
   > "Are you counting calories and following restrictive diets?"

2. **Why** -- Explain WHY it's a problem (what causes it)
   > "Those popular diets trigger your hunger hormones, causing metabolism to slow down"

3. **Agitate** -- Twist the knife. Make them FEEL the pain.
   > "And the worst part: if you're experiencing this hormone imbalance, it doesn't matter how hard you try -- you'll most likely fail."

4. **Solve** -- Present the solution + CTA
   > "To lose weight, you first need to fix the hormone imbalance. I just recorded a video explaining how... [link]"

> "A good copywriter not only stabs people with their problem, they also twist the knife."

---

## BAB (Before - After - Bridge)

Contains a built-in duality (before vs. after = problem vs. desire).

1. **Before** -- Paint current painful reality
2. **After** -- Paint desired reality
3. **Bridge** -- Your solution connects the two

Note: Less powerful than 6-Step because it doesn't reach internal/philosophical problem depth.

## Fill-in-the-Blanks VSL Template (3-5 min)

```
HOOK:
"If you are [TARGET CUSTOMER] who wants to [DESIRED OUTCOME]
but [PAIN POINT], watch out."

INTRODUCTION:
"Hi, my name is [NAME] and if you want to [DESIRED OUTCOME + BENEFIT]
without [PAIN POINT], pay close attention."

LIMITING BELIEF:
"Many [TARGET CUSTOMERS] currently suffer from the idea that
[LIMITING BELIEF related to pain point].
This idea leads to [CONSEQUENCES]."

VALIDATION:
"And as a result, most feel like [INTERNAL FRUSTRATION].
But it doesn't have to be that way."

SOLUTION TEASE (don't sell yet):
"If you want to [DESIRED OUTCOME + BENEFIT],
all you need is [SOLUTION CONCEPT, not your product yet]."

THROW ROCKS AT ENEMIES:
"Now I could go on for 50 minutes teasing you like everyone else
in this industry, but I'd rather just be upfront with you."

INTRODUCE SIGNATURE SOLUTION:
"I would like to introduce you to [SIGNATURE SOLUTION NAME].
It's a [system/protocol/program] that will allow you to
[DESIRED OUTCOME] without [PAIN POINT]."

REINFORCE (repetition = learning):
Keep hammering desired outcome + pain point removal.

SOCIAL PROOF:
"This is the same system our client [NAME] used to
[SPECIFIC RESULT] in [TIMEFRAME]."

CTA:
"If you want me to personally implement this in your life
during the next [TIMEFRAME], [CTA: book a call / buy / click]."
```

## The 4 Big Niches

1. **Money** -- business, career, side hustles, making money online
2. **Relationships** -- dating, marriage
3. **Health** -- fitness, weight loss, anti-aging
4. **Hobbies** -- pets, photography, sports, survival

Always think PROBLEM-FIRST, not product-first.

- "Truth is..."

- "The fact is..."
- "Here's the thing..."

- "And that's exactly why..."
- "In other words..."
- "Put simply..."
- "But it doesn't have to be that way."
- "Here's the good news..."
