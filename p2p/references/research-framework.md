# P2P Research Framework

## 3-Layer Problem Analysis

99% of copywriters never do this. From ONE external problem, derive all layers and uncover the real desire.

### Layer 1: External Problem
The problem people on the outside can see.

**Selection criteria:** Pick the one that:
- People experience MOST OFTEN in day-to-day life
- Is the MOST PRESSING and constant frustration
- Reminds them of the underlying issue every time

Examples:
- Weight loss: "Every time she takes a picture, she notices she looks overweight"
- Business: "Working a job they hate but can't quit"

### Layer 2: Internal Problem
How the external problem makes the person FEEL internally (emotional response).

Categories of internal problems:
- Insecurity & self-consciousness
- Shame & embarrassment
- Frustration & hopelessness
- Low self-esteem
- Loneliness & isolation
- Fear & anxiety

**Critical distinction:**
- External problem = rational level communication
- Internal problem = emotional level communication (THIS is how you hook people)

### Layer 3: Philosophical Problem (The Conflict)
The conflict/duality the internal problem creates inside the prospect.

- Weight loss: Avoids social gatherings to avoid pictures --> sacrificing social life because of weight
- Business: Wants online business, sees it's real, but doesn't know how --> duality of wanting and being unable

> "When you create conflict, you create movement."

Once aware of the philosophical problem, they CANNOT unsee it. They must fix it or live with it. If you've done a good job "stabbing and twisting," they can't live with it.

### Real Desire
The stated desire is NEVER the real desire:
- Says "lose 3 kilos" --> Real: self-worth, confidence, enjoying life without insecurity
- Says "$10K/month" --> Real: reclaim ownership of their life

## ChatGPT Prompting Method

### Step 1: Describe the Prospect
Give full context about who your ideal customer is. Demographics, situation, emotional state.

### Step 2: Explain Your Objective
"I'm trying to figure out what problems related to [X] this person experiences day-to-day."

### Step 3: Give the Specific Task
"Give me a list of real problems they experience daily. The more real and relatable, the better."

**Rules:**
- Talk to AI like a 10-year-old: give ALL details, full context
- Keep everything in ONE conversation thread (context accumulates)
- Push further after first output ("I want even more real scenarios")
- Use GPT-4 / Claude for best results

## 2 Copy Emotions

Every claim can be framed two ways:

| Moving Toward Pleasure | Moving Away from Pain/Fear |
|---|---|
| "Become confident in social situations" | "Stop feeling anxious in social situations" |
| "Take pictures and be proud" | "Stop looking overweight in pictures" |
| Feels OPTIONAL | Feels like NO OPTION -- you MUST act |

**Pain is 2x more powerful than pleasure.** Always create BOTH versions of every claim.

---

## Rock Bottom vs Top of the Mountain

### Rock Bottom
Worst possible scenario if they do nothing -- ALL fears come true. Describe life in vivid detail.

### Top of the Mountain
Ideal state after transformation -- ALL desires achieved. Describe life in vivid detail.

### Naming Guidelines
Create memorable, self-explanatory names:
- Business: "Master of your own future" vs. "Slave of your fears"
- Weight loss: "Picture-perfect fit" vs. "Prisoner of the plate"
- Male self-dev: "Top 1% men" vs. "Modern-day slave"

Requirements: self-explanatory, tangible/concrete, related to day-to-day life, aspirational (top) or fearful (bottom).

## Hot Keywords + 5 Angle Methods

### Identifying Hot Keywords
Every niche has emotionally charged words that permeate all conversations. Identify 10-20 yourself first, then use AI to supplement.

Weight loss examples: diet, starvation, hunger, weight, exercise, scale, confidence, body image, food, self-esteem, clothing, photos, fitness, judgment

### 5 Angle Methods (for each keyword, create narratives)

| # | Method | Description | Example |
|---|--------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | News Effect | Make it sound like a new discovery | "New research suggests bodies have a weight set point" |
| 2 | Question Existing Truths | Challenge accepted beliefs (contrarian) | "Is the no-carb trend actually hindering weight loss?" |
| 3 | Show New Perspectives | Familiar thing, different angle | "The Zoom Effect on body image" |
| 4 | Enhance/Amplify Real Problem | Go deeper into fears/desires | "Why starvation diets don't work" |
| 5 | Create Own Theories | Most powerful -- your unique beliefs | Your brand's unique perspective |

## 9 Proven Email Angle Templates

1. **"This situation means your fear is coming true"** -- Very aggressive but effective
2. **"What you suspect IS happening"** -- Confirms suspicions, strong bonding
3. **"You're right about X, and that's a problem"** -- Validates then redirects
4. **"Fix your problem without anyone knowing"** -- Best for shame problems
5. **"Facing this? Here's what to do"** -- Simple, direct
6. **"The logical solution only makes it worse"** -- Contrarian, sets you apart
7. **"It's not your fault. Do this to fix it"** -- Externalizing blame = extremely powerful
8. **"If you have this problem, do this"** -- Simple conditional
9. **"Why the conventional method doesn't work"** -- Classic contrarian

### 5 Fundamental Principles Behind These Angles
1. **Confirming Suspicions** -- People love being told they were right
2. **Contrarian Approach** -- Different = attention + hook
3. **News-Related** -- "New research shows..." + scientific claims
4. **Best-Kept Secret / Hidden Gem** -- Most powerful and impactful type
5. **Emotional Stories** -- Especially combined with contrarian or confirming suspicions

## Research Tools

### 1. YouTube Comments
Search your exact topic, read ALL comments. Look for: pain points in their own words, specific situations, exact language.

### 2. Facebook Ad Library
Search keywords, browse running ads. Ads running repeatedly = profitable. Model what works.

### 3. ClickBank
Sort by Gravity (high to low). Analyze sales pages: claims, structures, angles. Model proven approaches.

### 4. Customer Surveys
Key questions: main frustration, main obstacle, current situation, goals, what they like/dislike. Direct quotes become hooks and sales arguments.
